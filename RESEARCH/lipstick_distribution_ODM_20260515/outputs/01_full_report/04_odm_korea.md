# Section 4: Korean ODM Manufacturing Landscape

## 4.1 Industry Overview

South Korea's ODM cosmetics ecosystem — anchored by Cosmax and Kolmar Korea — is globally recognized as the most innovation-efficient supply chain for beauty products. Both companies surpassed **KRW 2 trillion (~USD 1.4B) in annual revenue** in 2024, a milestone termed the "2 trillion won club" [src_020]. The total Korea Cosmetics ODM market is forecast to grow significantly through 2035 [src_022].

The Korean ODM model democratized cosmetics brand creation: brands do not need in-house chemistry or manufacturing — they commission ODMs who maintain **extensive libraries of ready-made formulas** and execute fast product cycles, often **from concept to shelf in less than six months** [src_019].

## 4.2 Key Manufacturers

### Cosmax (코스맥스)

**Profile:**
- Founded 1992; global largest cosmetics ODM by output
- 2024 revenue: approximately **USD 1.2 billion (KRW ~1.7 trillion)**; first Korean ODM to exceed KRW 2 trillion in production capacity terms [src_020, src_023]
- Manufactures for **300–600+ global beauty brands** worldwide [search result]
- Facilities: South Korea (Pyeongtaek main plant, capacity ~130M units/year), China (since 2004), USA, Indonesia, Thailand — total global capacity **>1.7 billion units/year** [search result]
- R&D: 1,300 staff across global R&D centers; invests ~5–7% of revenue in R&D annually [src_023]

**Lipstick Capabilities:**
- Full formulation range: cream lipstick, matte lipstick, liquid lip tint, glossy lip oil, cushion lip, balm stick, lip gloss, hybrid lip-skincare products
- 70% of Cosmax ODM catalog features vegan-certified base formulas [src_021]
- Proprietary polymer systems for long-wear technology [src_021]

**Lipstick ODM Terms (approximate):**
- MOQ (stock ODM — from existing formula library): **3,000 units per shade** [src_021]
- MOQ (custom modification): **10,000 units** [src_021]
- Lead time: **9–11 weeks from PO to delivery** [src_021]
- Cost per unit: **USD 2.40–4.50** (varies by packaging complexity) [src_021]
- Engagement: Initial consultation → formula selection from library or brief → sampling → regulatory review → production → QC → delivery

*Note: Cosmax MOQ/lead time data sourced from ShelfTrend industry guide [src_021]. Cosmax does not publicly publish pricing; brand-specific terms vary by volume and relationship.*

## 4.3 Engagement Process: New Brand Entering Korean ODM Partnership

Based on industry standard practice [src_019, src_021]:

1. **Initial Contact & NDA:** Brand contacts ODM directly (website, Korea cosmetics trade shows like CosmoProf Asia/Beautyworld). NDA signed before formula disclosure.

2. **Brief Submission:** Brand submits product brief (product type, texture, finish, target market, regulatory requirements, packaging vision, budget, timeline, target retail price).

3. **Formula Consultation:** ODM presents existing formula library options (faster, lower cost) or proposes custom development (higher MOQ, longer timeline, additional development fees).

4. **Sampling:** ODM produces initial samples (typically 2–5 rounds). Lab dip colors reviewed against brand brief.

5. **Regulatory Alignment:** ODM conducts ingredient review for target market regulations (MFDS for Korea, FDA for US, EC 1223/2009 for EU). Required testing coordinated.

6. **Commercial Terms:** MOQ, unit pricing, payment terms (typically 30–50% deposit, balance on delivery), lead time agreed.

7. **Production:** Master batch → pilot run → full production → QC inspection.

8. **Delivery & Compliance:** Products shipped with COA (Certificate of Analysis), MSDS, and regulatory documentation.

**Typical Total Timeline (first product):** 4–9 months from initial contact to goods-in-hand, depending on formula novelty and regulatory complexity [src_019].

## 4.5 IP Protection Considerations

The Korean ODM model maintains a **"shared infrastructure, proprietary insight"** model [src_019]:
- ODM retains ownership of base formulas in their library
- **Brand's specific insight** (target ingredient story, shade palette, positioning) is treated as proprietary
- NDAs are standard; however, formula libraries are shared across multiple brand clients
- For truly proprietary formulations (custom development with exclusive use clause), brands must negotiate exclusivity terms — typically requiring higher MOQ or exclusivity fees
- Brands that want full IP ownership of formulas must engage in OEM model (brand owns the formula, ODM manufactures to spec) — this requires the brand to provide its own formula (often developed with an independent cosmetic chemist) or pay for full custom development with IP transfer clause

**Risk:** Because ODMs serve many clients from a shared formula library, brand differentiation must come from ingredients, positioning, shades, and packaging — not formula secrecy. This is the standard K-beauty "trinity" model [src_019].
