# S3 — Lipstick History & Category Evolution Timeline

## 1. Ancient Origins (c. 3500 BCE – 500 CE)

### Mesopotamia / Sumer (c. 3500–3000 BCE)
- Sumerians are credited as among the earliest documented users of lip color, crushing gemstones and minerals (including white lead mixed with red rock) to create pigmented pastes for the lips. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842) [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- Queen Puabi of Ur (c. 2600 BCE) is among the earliest named individuals linked to lip color use, found buried with cosmetic implements. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- Lipstick in this era was a marker of elite status and power, not mass beauty practice. [(Wikipedia – Lipstick)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

### Indus Valley (c. 3000 BCE)
- Rectangular ochre sticks used to decorate lips; crushed semi-precious stones applied to lips and eyes. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842) [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

### Ancient Egypt (c. 3100 BCE onward)
- Ceremonial cosmetic palettes dated to 3100 BCE used for grinding pigments. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- Common formula: red ochre mixed with resin or animal fat. Cleopatra VII used crushed carmine beetles and ants for deep red pigment. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick) [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)
- Nefertiti's bust (c. 1345 BCE) depicts red-painted lips as an established beauty ideal. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)
- Egyptian women also used seaweed with bromine mannite and iodine as a red dye for lips. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)

### Ancient Greece
- Lip paint was strongly associated with sex workers; Greek law began regulating cosmetics on this basis — one of the earliest documented cases of regulatory control over cosmetics. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick) [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- Ingredients included mulberries, seaweed, sheep sweat, and crocodile excrement. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)

### Ancient Rome
- Lip color indicated social rank; worn by both men and women of the upper class.
- The pigment vermillion (mercury-based) was fashionable but noted as a "potentially deadly poison." [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

### China / Tang Dynasty (618–907 CE)
- Chinese developed beeswax-based formulas with scented oils. Tang-era performers used black or red lip paint corresponding to stage character roles. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick) [(Type Beauty)](https://www.typebeautyinc.com/en-us/blogs/blog/history-of-lipstick-how-iconic-cosmetic-evolved)

### Japan
- Geishas applied safflower petal pigment to the lips in a small bow shape, a distinct aesthetic tradition. [(Type Beauty)](https://www.typebeautyinc.com/en-us/blogs/blog/history-of-lipstick-how-iconic-cosmetic-evolved)

### Mesoamerica
- Mayan women used lip paint; evidence documented in The Grolier Codex (13th century CE). [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)

## 2. Medieval Period (c. 500–1400 CE)

- The Christian Church actively condemned cosmetics as vain and ungodly; women wearing makeup were considered "an incarnation of Satan" in parts of medieval England. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)
- In practice: many women continued using rose rouge made from sheep fat and mashed red roots. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)
- 1200s Italy: high-society ladies wore bright pink lip rouge; lower-class women used earthy red shades — color served as a social class marker. [(Ancient Origins)](https://www.ancient-origins.net/history-ancient-traditions/brief-history-enduring-iconic-female-phenomenon-red-lipstick-007842)
- Arab physician Abulcasis (c. 936–1013 CE) is credited with inventing the solid lipstick — a pressed formulation. [(Mae Polzine)](https://maepolzine.com/blog/the-history-of-lipstick/)
- The Crusades (1095–1291) reintroduced Western Europeans to Middle Eastern cosmetic practices. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)

---

## 3. Renaissance (15th–17th Century)

- Queen Elizabeth I of England (reign 1558–1603) made red lips a fashion statement among English aristocrats. Her custom formula: cochineal, gum arabic, egg whites, and fig milk. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- Pale face + bright red lips became the Elizabethan beauty ideal; restricted largely to nobility and stage performers. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- Under King James I (1603–1625), cosmetics were again stigmatized — associated with witchcraft. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- 1770: England passed legislation allowing women who used makeup to "trick men into marriage" to be prosecuted as witches. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)

---

## 4. 19th Century — First Commercial Lipstick

- **1870**: Paris-based Maison Guerlain began developing an early commercial lip product. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **1884**: Guerlain produced the first mass-market commercial lipstick — a stick wrapped in silk paper, made from deer tallow, castor oil, and beeswax. Applied with the finger or a brush; no tube yet. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick) [(Mae Polzine)](https://maepolzine.com/blog/the-history-of-lipstick/)
- 1880s: Actress Sarah Bernhardt wore carmine-dyed lipstick publicly, beginning to shift social stigma. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

---

## 5. Early 20th Century (1900–1940)

- **1912**: Elizabeth Arden distributed red lipstick tubes to suffragettes marching down Fifth Avenue — one of the earliest uses of lipstick as political symbol. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick)
- **1915**: Maurice Levy invented the first cylindrical metal container for lipstick with a push-tab mechanism. [(Mae Polzine)](https://maepolzine.com/blog/the-history-of-lipstick/)
- **1923**: James Bruce Mason Jr. patented the swivel-up (twist-up) lipstick tube — the format still used today. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **1928**: Max Factor created "Lip Pomade" for film sets — a sheer, high-shine formula. This is the origin of lip gloss. [(Medusa's Makeup)](https://medusasmakeup.com/blogs/news/the-shiny-evolution-a-history-of-lip-gloss)
- 1920s: Silent film actress Clara Bow popularized the "Cupid's bow" lip shape. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

---

## 6. Mid-20th Century (1940–1970)

- **WWII**: Red lipstick became a patriotic act — shades like "Fighting Red!" and "Victory Red!" were marketed to boost civilian morale. Elizabeth Arden created a matching lipstick for the U.S. Marine Corps Women's Reserve uniform. [(National Geographic)](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/history-of-red-lipstick) [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **1950**: Hazel Bishop created "No-Smear" lipstick — the first long-lasting formula, using bromo acid staining dyes. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **1951**: Revlon released lipsticks color-matched to nail polish shades. 98% of American women reported wearing lipstick regularly. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- Bullet lipstick format became the industry norm during this era.
- **1973**: Bonnie Bell launched Lip Smacker — flavored lip gloss targeting the teen market. [(Mae Polzine)](https://maepolzine.com/blog/the-history-of-lipstick/)

---

## 7. Late 20th Century (1970s–1990s)

- 1970s–1980s: Black lipstick through punk/goth subcultures; iridescent and frosted shades entered mainstream. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **1984**: MAC Cosmetics (Frank Toskan and Frank Angelo, Toronto) launched — credited with developing the first modern matte lipstick formula for professional artists. [(StyleCraze)](https://www.stylecraze.com/articles/a-complete-history-of-lipstick/)
- 1990s: Dark lip liner + lighter lipstick combination became a defining trend. Madonna's use of MAC Russian Red cemented the brand. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)

---

## 8. 2000s — The Gloss Era

- The 2000s saw a pivot to lip gloss as the dominant lip format: Lancôme Juicy Tubes, MAC Lipglass, Stila Lip Glazes. [(Nylon)](https://www.nylon.com/beauty/the-evolution-of-the-it-girl-lip-color)
- Classic bullet lipstick was commercially depressed. Light pink, nude, and frosted shades dominated.

---

## 9. 2010s — Matte Liquid Lipstick & Social Media

- Matte liquid lipstick emerged as a distinct category: transfer-resistant, full-coverage liquid with flat-matte dry-down.
- **2015**: Kylie Lip Kit launched — matte liquid lipstick + matching liner; cultural inflection point for the category. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **2012**: Taylor Swift's album "Red" correlated with measurable sales increase in red lipstick. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- Late 2010s: K-beauty lip tints (gradient/ombre, water-tint formulas) entered global awareness; K-beauty exports to the US expanded sharply 2015–2019.

---

## 10. 2020s — Category Fragmentation & Skinification

- COVID-19 (2020–2021): Mask-wearing significantly reduced lipstick sales globally; nude and tinted-balm formats partially sustained the category. [(Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipstick)
- **2021–2022**: Dior Lip Glow Oil drove breakout for lip oil as a standalone category. [(Yahoo/Dior)](https://shopping.yahoo.com/deals/articles/not-drill-dior-lip-oil-090000748.html)
- Lip plumpers transitioned from novelty to mainstream category in the early-to-mid 2020s.
- Tinted lip treatments (balm + tint + serum hybrids): 60%+ growth as of 2025 data. [(Glossy.co)](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/read-my-lips-how-lip-products-saved-makeup-in-2025/)
- Skinification trend: products marketed with niacinamide, vitamin C, peptides, SPF. [(Type Beauty)](https://www.typebeautyinc.com/en-us/blogs/blog/history-of-lipstick-how-iconic-cosmetic-evolved)

---

## 11. Current State (2024–2026): Classic Lipstick

- **Lip liner resurgence**: sales grew 28% YTD as of October 2025, the strongest growth in the overall lip category. [(Glossy.co)](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/read-my-lips-how-lip-products-saved-makeup-in-2025/)
- Sculpted outer edge + plumped gloss dominates the prestige market in 2024–2025. [(Glossy.co)](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/read-my-lips-how-lip-products-saved-makeup-in-2025/)
- Prestige makeup overall grew 1% in H1 2025 (Circana data); lip products are the category's strongest performer. [(Glossy.co)](https://www.glossy.co/beauty/read-my-lips-how-lip-products-saved-makeup-in-2025/)
- Classic lipstick is no longer the dominant lip format; the market is fragmented across gloss, tint, oil, plumper, tinted treatment, liner, and hybrid SKUs.
