# S1+S2 — Lipstick Formulation Types & Ingredient Structure

## S1. Lipstick Formulation Types

### 1.1 Finish Type Definitions and Technical Mechanisms

#### Matte
- **Definition**: Zero-shine, full-drama finish with maximum color opacity and no light reflection.
- **Technical mechanism**: High wax concentration + oil-absorbing powders (silica, kaolin clay, starches) that physically absorb surface oils and scatter light rather than reflect it. Reduced oil content prevents the refractive index from creating shine.
- **Key trade-off**: "The use of clays and silicas results in a hard stick which becomes increasingly dry and hard when the solid substance content increases" (US Patent US20200214950A1, cited in pharmacy180.com).
- **Longevity**: Highest among all finish types. Oil-free or low-oil formula grips lips and resists transfer.
- **Consumer use cases**: Bold, professional, long-wear looks; editorial makeup; occasions where touch-ups are impossible.
- Source: [typebeautyinc.com — Understanding Lipstick Formulations](https://www.typebeautyinc.com/blogs/blog/understanding-lipstick-formulations) (2024); [ibacosmetics.com](https://ibacosmetics.com/blogs/iba-blogs/creamy-lipstick-vs-matte-lipstick)

#### Creamy (Cream)
- **Definition**: Full-coverage pigment with a soft, low sheen and moisturizing feel. Richer emollient content than satin.
- **Technical mechanism**: Higher oil and butter content (shea butter, castor oil) balanced with waxes. Less silica/kaolin vs. matte.
- **Consumer use cases**: Everyday wear, dry lip conditions, users wanting color payoff with comfort.
- Source: [typebeautyinc.com](https://www.typebeautyinc.com/blogs/blog/understanding-lipstick-formulations); [maccosmetics.co.uk](https://www.maccosmetics.co.uk/ask-makeup-artist/lips/what-is-the-difference-between-matte-and-cream-lipstick)

#### Satin
- **Definition**: Semi-matte finish with a soft luminous sheen — positioned between matte and glossy.
- **Technical mechanism**: Moderate oil content (more than matte, less than gloss) combined with medium wax concentration. The soft-focus sheen comes from controlled light reflection.
- **Consumer use cases**: Daywear, versatile everyday formula.
- Source: [lipstickqueen.com — Matte vs Satin](https://lipstickqueen.com/matte-vs-satin-lipstick/); [beautylish.com](https://www.beautylish.com/a/vcawy/guide-to-lipstick-finishes-and-textures)

#### Metallic / Frost
- **Definition**: High-shine finish with directional light reflection; jewel-toned with gold, silver, or bronze shimmer.
- **Technical mechanism**: Micro-fine mica particles (and/or bismuth oxychloride, guanine crystals) suspended in a creamy base. "Frosted and metallic lipsticks get their glow from micro-fine mica particles that reflect light."
- **Consumer use cases**: Evening/event wear, editorial, trend-driven looks.
- Source: [beautylish.com](https://www.beautylish.com/a/vcawy/guide-to-lipstick-finishes-and-textures); [qcmakeupacademy.com](https://www.qcmakeupacademy.com/blog/2016/12/lipstick-finishes-101)

#### Sheer / Balm
- **Definition**: Translucent wash of color; tinted lip balm territory with minimal pigment load.
- **Technical mechanism**: Very low pigment concentration (2–5% vs. 15–25% in full-coverage), high emollient content.
- **Consumer use cases**: No-makeup makeup looks, layering over liner, casual/natural wear.
- Source: [beautylish.com](https://www.beautylish.com/a/vcawy/guide-to-lipstick-finishes-and-textures)

#### Gloss
- **Definition**: High-shine, wet-looking finish with reflective optical effect.
- **Technical mechanism**: Maximum oil and emollient concentration (polybutene, mineral oil, silicone oils) raising the film's refractive index. "There is an excellent correlation between the glossiness of oils and their refractive index (R² = 0.995)." Phenyl Trimethicone delivers shine without stickiness.
- **Consumer use cases**: Plumping visual effect, luminous casual looks, layering.
- Source: [ocl-journal.org — Functional Roles of Lipids (2020, peer-reviewed)](https://www.ocl-journal.org/articles/ocl/full_html/2020/01/ocl200042/ocl200042.html)

### 1.2 Emerging / Newer Formulation Types (2023–2026)

| Type | Description | Key Technology |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydrating Matte** | Non-drying matte delivering 24-hour wear | Hyaluronic acid, shea butter in traditional matte matrix; smart oil-absorbing polymers |
| **Liquid Matte (Long-Wear)** | Liquid-to-matte film-forming formula | Acrylates copolymers, trimethylsiloxysilicate, isododecane solvent |
| **Transfer-Proof / Zero-Transfer** | Budge-proof color with polyurethane-silicone hybrid film | 40% better color retention vs. conventional matte |
| **Skincare-Infused** | Lip treatment + color hybrid | SPF, antioxidants, peptides, collagen boosters integrated |
| **Clean/Vegan Matte** | Organic formulas without synthetic waxes | Candelilla replaces beeswax; plant-derived oils replace mineral/petrolatum |

## S2. Lipstick Ingredient Structure

### 2.1 Core Ingredient Categories and Typical Concentrations

| Category | Typical % | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Waxes | 8–18% (stick); lower in liquid | Structure, hardness, melting point control |
| Oils / Emollients | 30–55% | Pigment dispersion, spreadability, gloss, moisture |
| Waxy Pastes / Butters | ~20% | Balance hardness/softness, skin feel |
| Colorants (pigments + dyes) | 8–25% | Color payoff and opacity |
| Texturing Powders | 5–15% (matte-heavy) | Matte effect (silica, kaolin); shimmer (mica, bismuth oxychloride) |
| Polymers / Film-formers | 0.2–2% | Adhesion, wear resistance, transfer resistance |
| Preservatives | ~1% | Microbial protection |
| Antioxidants | trace | Rancidity prevention |
| Perfume | 1–3% | Mask ingredient odor |

Source: [labmuffin.com — What's in a Lipstick](https://labmuffin.com/whats-in-a-lipstick/); [pharmacy180.com — Formulation of Lipsticks](https://www.pharmacy180.com/article/formulation-of-lipsticks-815/)

### 2.2 Waxes: Detailed Roles

#### Beeswax
- **Source**: Animal-derived (honeybees). Not vegan. Melting point: ~63°C. Concentration: 3–10%
- **Role**: Classical stiffening agent. "Extensively used for entrapping castor oil." Provides firmness, film strength, locks in color. Creates a film on application.

#### Carnauba Wax
- **Source**: Leaf wax from *Copernicia prunifera* (Brazil). Vegan. Melting point: 81–90°C (highest of common lipstick waxes). Concentration: 3–5%
- **Role**: "One of the hardest natural waxes" — raises melting point and increases rigidity. Prevents lipstick from melting in warm climates. Adds glossy sheen.

#### Candelilla Wax
- **Source**: Derived from *Euphorbia antisyphilitica* shrub. Vegan. Melting point: 65–75°C. Concentration: 9–15%
- **Role**: Lighter and more pliable than carnauba. Primary beeswax substitute in vegan formulas (note: ~twice the stiffening power of beeswax, so used at half the amount).

#### Ozokerite / Ceresin
- **Source**: Mineral (petroleum-derived) or synthetic. Melting point: 61–78°C. Concentration: 3–10%
- **Role**: Precise control over hardness and melting behavior. Often used alongside beeswax/carnauba to fine-tune final stick hardness.

Source: [naturalbulksupplies.com — Guide to Cosmetic Waxes](https://naturalbulksupplies.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-cosmetic-waxes-beeswax-vegan-alternatives-and-specialty-waxes-explained/); [pharmacy180.com](https://www.pharmacy180.com/article/formulation-of-lipsticks-815/); [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov — Meadowfoam seed oil (2022, peer-reviewed)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9135126/)

### 2.3 Oils: Detailed Roles

#### Castor Oil
- **Chemical profile**: ~90% ricinoleic acid (C18 hydroxyl fatty acid). Concentration: Can reach 40–54% of total formula; typically #1 ingredient by weight.
- **Role**: Primary pigment dispersant ("very good solvent for pigment wetting and grinding"), wax solubilizer, viscosity contributor. Also directly contributes gloss through its high refractive index. Castor oil dispersions are "more viscous, stickier and harder to spread" — provides adhesion to lips.
- **Note**: Higher castor oil = glossier, stickier formula; reducing it creates less-glossy, lighter-wear results.

#### Jojoba Oil
- **Chemical profile**: Technically a liquid wax ester (~97% C20–C22 straight-chain esters)
- **Role**: Emollient, oxidative stability, skin-conditioning. Lighter feel than castor oil. Used in modern clean-beauty formulations.

#### Silicone Oils (Dimethicone, Phenyl Trimethicone)
- **Role**: Phenyl Trimethicone delivers "shine to formulations without adding any stickiness." Prevalent from the 1970s onward.

#### Fatty Esters (Isostearyl Isostearate, Ethylhexyl Palmitate, Isopropyl Maleate)
- **Role**: Lighter emollients that enhance spreadability and gloss without the heaviness of castor oil.

Source: [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9135126/) (2022); [pharmacy180.com](https://www.pharmacy180.com/article/formulation-of-lipsticks-815/); [ocl-journal.org](https://www.ocl-journal.org/articles/ocl/full_html/2020/01/ocl200042/ocl200042.html) (2020)

### 2.4 Polymers / Film-Formers (0.2–2%)

- **Key types**:
  - Acrylates/C12–22 alkylmethacrylate copolymers: provide adhesion to lips
  - Trimethylsiloxysilicate (MQ resin): silicone-based film former in transfer-proof formulas
  - Polypropylsilsesquioxane + silicone acrylate copolymer: newer hybrid for comfortable long-wear
- **Mechanism**: Form a flexible, invisible film over lip color after application. A large-scale polymer provides film adhesion/flexibility; a finer-branched polymer creates a 3D network that traps colorant dyes, preventing transfer.
- **Matte liquid lipstick**: Depends heavily on film-forming polymers in a volatile solvent (isododecane) base — solvent evaporates post-application, leaving the polymer-color film behind.

Source: [rawsource.com — Liquid Lipstick Formulation](https://rawsource.com/how-liquid-lipstick-formulation-is-made/)

### 2.5 Pigment Types

#### Inorganic Pigments: Iron Oxides
- **CI numbers**: Red (CI 77491), Yellow (CI 77492), Black (CI 77499)
- **Properties**: Non-toxic, non-irritating, FDA-approved. High stability to heat and light (superior to organic pigments). Produce brown, tan, nude, and warm-red shades.
- **Note**: Iron oxides are insoluble particles — remain suspended in the wax/oil matrix, not dissolved.

#### Organic Pigments / Lakes
- **Definition**: Lakes are made by precipitating a water-soluble dye (e.g., D&C Red No. 7) onto an insoluble mineral substrate (alumina hydrate, barium oxide).
- **Key lake pigments in lips**: D&C Red No. 7 Lake (CI 15850:1), D&C Red No. 6 Lake, D&C Red No. 27 Lake
- **Properties**: Bright, vibrant, intense color intensity (superior to inorganic). Less stable to light and heat than iron oxides.

#### Titanium Dioxide (TiO₂)
- **Role**: Opacifying and brightening agent. Added to increase opacity in lighter shades and pastel formulas.

#### Mica / Bismuth Oxychloride / Guanine
- **Role**: Pearlescent and metallic effect pigments. Mica's flat platelet structure creates specular (directional) light reflection — the metallic shimmer effect.

#### Soluble Dyes (Bromo Acids / pH-Sensitive Dyes)
- **Examples**: Eosin (D&C Red No. 21), carmine; newer pH-reactive dyes in "mood" or "color-changing" lipsticks
- **Mechanism**: Water-soluble; dissolve fully into the formulation. pH-sensitive dyes change color on application due to individual lip pH differences. Unlike pigments, dyes can stain lip tissue.

Source: [sensient-beauty.com — Dye or Pigment](https://sensient-beauty.com/insights/dye-or-pigment-using-the-right-ingredients-in-makeup-formulation/); [dornsife.usc.edu/scribe — Truth of Pigments (Feb 2026)](https://dornsife.usc.edu/scribe/2026/02/27/the-truth-of-pigments-how-iron-oxides-lakes-and-natural-pigments-shape-color-cosmetics/)

### 2.6 Formulation Differences: Matte vs. Creamy vs. Long-Wear

| Parameter | Matte | Creamy/Satin | Long-Wear Liquid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wax % | High (15–20%) | Medium (10–15%) | Low or none (liquid base) |
| Oil % | Low | High (30–55%) | Very low (volatile solvent: isododecane) |
| Castor oil | Reduced or absent | Dominant (40–54%) | Minimal |
| Silica/Kaolin | High (5–15%) | Low or absent | Low |
| Polymers | Low–moderate | Low | High (acrylate copolymers, silicone resins) |
| Pearlescents (mica) | Absent | Absent (unless metallic) | Absent |
| Titanium dioxide | High (opacity) | Medium | High |
| Pigment load | Very high | Medium–high | High |
| Finish mechanism | Oil absorption + light scattering | Refractive index of oils creates soft sheen | Film-forming polymer locks pigment; volatile base evaporates |
